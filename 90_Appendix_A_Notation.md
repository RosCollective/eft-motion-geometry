# Appendix A: Notation

This appendix collects the minimal notation used throughout the manuscript. The system is geometric rather than algebraic, so notation is descriptive and structural rather than symbolic.

## A.1 Field-Level Symbols

- **F** — the field (the relational space in which motion occurs)  
- **M** — motion (continuity across turns)  
- **C** — curvature (deviation of motion)  
- **K** — collapse (dimensional reduction)  
- **R** — reconstitution (return to coherence)  
- **A** — alignment (mobility compatibility)  
- **S** — stability (sustained coherence)

These symbols are not variables. They are structural markers.

## A.2 Ecological Conditions

- **O₂** — oxygen (linguistic spaciousness)  
- **H** — altitude (vantage above local turns)  
- **E** — ecology (environmental support)  
- **T** — triadic configuration (stabilizing third element)

These conditions determine whether the field can sustain motion.

## A.3 Curvature Indicators

- **C⁺** — positive curvature (coherence-enhancing)  
- **C⁻** — negative curvature (collapse-adjacent)  
- **Cᵒ** — oscillatory curvature (boundary condition)

Curvature is diagnostic.

## A.4 Collapse Signatures

- **K₁** — tightening curvature  
- **K₂** — oscillatory motion  
- **K₃** — fragmentation of alignment  
- **K₄** — loss of dimensionality  

These signatures appear before collapse.

## A.5 Reconstitution Pathway

- **R₁** — curvature return  
- **R₂** — widening motion  
- **R₃** — alignment restoration  
- **R₄** — coherence rebuilding  
- **R₅** — stability re-establishment  

Reconstitution is sequential.

## A.6 Structural Relationships

- **M → C** — motion reveals curvature  
- **C → K** — curvature predicts collapse  
- **K → R** — collapse enables reconstitution  
- **R → S** — reconstitution leads to stability  
- **S → M** — stability supports motion  

These relationships form the geometry of the field.
