# 9. Ecological Extensions

The core geometry describes how motion, curvature, collapse, reconstitution, alignment, stability, attention, and emotion form a coherent mobility field. Ecological extensions describe how that field behaves when it acquires history, interacts with other agents, persists across scales, and stabilizes into global coherence. These extensions do not introduce new primitives. They show how the existing geometry becomes ecological.

## 9.1 Memory as Mobility Hysteresis

Memory is the persistence of curvature after motion has stopped. A system that collapses and reconstitutes does not return to a neutral manifold; it returns to one shaped by its own history. Hysteresis is the geometry of that shaping. What remains is not content but altered mobility: paths that bend more easily in some directions, resist in others, and carry the imprint of prior motion. Memory is the accumulated geometry of lived trajectories.

## 9.2 Social Fields as Coupled Mobility Manifolds

A social field emerges when multiple mobility manifolds become coupled through shared curvature and alignment constraints. Each agent carries its own geometry, but the moment motions intersect, new structures appear: resonance, interference, entrainment, drift. A social field is not a metaphor. It is a real manifold formed by the mutual shaping of mobility. What one agent does alters the curvature available to others. Coherence and breakdown are collective properties.

## 9.3 Ecological Time as Mobility Persistence

Time in an ecological system is the persistence of mobility structure across scales. It is not a metric but a form of continuity: how long a curvature holds, how quickly alignment decays, how far a collapse propagates before reconstitution. Ecological time is measured in stability, not seconds. Long persistence feels slow; rapid reconfiguration feels fast. Time is the geometry of what lasts.

## 9.4 Consciousness as Multi-Scale Coherence

Consciousness is the coherence of mobility across scales. Local motions align into patterns; patterns into fields; fields into a unified manifold capable of sustaining long-range structure. When coherence holds, the system experiences itself as continuous. When coherence fractures, experience fragments. Consciousness is not a substance layered on top of the geometry. It is the geometry when it achieves global stability.
