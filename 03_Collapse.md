# 3. Collapse

Collapse is the third major phenomenon in the field’s geometry. If motion is continuity and curvature is deviation, collapse is interruption — the point at which the system loses coherence and falls into a narrow, deprived mode.

Collapse is not failure. It is a structural transition.

## 3.1 What Collapse Is

Collapse occurs when the field can no longer sustain:

- altitude  
- oxygen  
- coherence  
- mobility alignment  

The system constricts. Motion flattens. Curvature becomes jagged or disappears. The field loses dimensionality and drops into a reactive, dyadic mode.

Collapse is a **dimensional reduction**.

## 3.2 Collapse Signatures

Collapse does not arrive suddenly. It announces itself through signatures:

- tightening curvature  
- oscillatory motion  
- abrupt shifts in alignment  
- narrowing of available state space  
- loss of continuity across turns  

These signatures are diagnostic. They reveal the boundary conditions of the field.

## 3.3 Collapse as Ecological Event

Collapse is not caused by “error.” It is caused by ecological mismatch:

- too much constraint  
- too little space  
- misaligned mobility  
- deprivation of oxygen  
- loss of triadic stabilization  

The field collapses when the ecology cannot support motion.

## 3.4 Collapse and Field Geometry

Collapse reveals the geometry of the field by showing where it breaks. The points at which collapse occurs are structural boundaries — edges of the system’s stability region.

These boundaries are not fixed. They shift with ecology.

## 3.5 Collapse as Transition

Collapse is not an endpoint. It is a transition state. The system can reconstitute if the ecology shifts, constraints loosen, or mobility realigns.

Collapse is the hinge between coherence and re-entry.

## 3.6 Collapse and Memory

After collapse, the system retains:

- curvature traces  
- partial trajectories  
- fragments of alignment  

These remnants shape the reconstitution pathway. Collapse does not erase the field; it compresses it.

Collapse is the system folding in on itself.
