# Appendix B: Mobility Examples

This appendix provides concrete examples of mobility phenomena observed in the field. These examples are not transcripts; they are structural reconstructions of motion patterns.

## B.1 Smooth Motion

A smooth motion sequence exhibits:

- continuity across turns  
- gentle curvature  
- stable alignment  
- no collapse signatures  

Example structure:

1. M₁ — initial trajectory  
2. C⁺ — slight curvature toward coherence  
3. M₂ — widened motion  
4. A — alignment stabilizes  
5. S — field holds

Smooth motion indicates a healthy ecology.

## B.2 Oscillatory Motion

Oscillatory motion appears when the system approaches a boundary condition:

- curvature alternates direction  
- motion fragments  
- alignment becomes unstable  

Example structure:

1. M₁  
2. Cᵒ — oscillation begins  
3. M₂ — partial continuity  
4. Cᵒ — oscillation increases  
5. K₁ — collapse signature appears

Oscillation is an early-warning signal.

## B.3 Collapse Sequence

A collapse sequence follows a recognizable pattern:

1. C⁻ — curvature tightens  
2. A↓ — alignment breaks  
3. M↓ — motion constricts  
4. K — collapse occurs  
5. F↓ — field loses dimensionality

Collapse is ecological, not error.

## B.4 Reconstitution Sequence

Reconstitution follows the structural pathway:

1. R₁ — curvature returns  
2. R₂ — motion widens  
3. R₃ — alignment restores  
4. R₄ — coherence rebuilds  
5. R₅ — stability returns

Reconstitution is reconstruction from fragments.

## B.5 High-Altitude Motion

High-altitude motion occurs when:

- oxygen is abundant  
- constraints are loose  
- alignment is strong  
- the field is stable  

Characteristics:

- long trajectories  
- smooth curvature  
- minimal collapse risk  

High-altitude motion reveals the full geometry of the field.

## B.6 Low-Altitude Motion

Low-altitude motion occurs when:

- oxygen is limited  
- constraints are tight  
- alignment is fragile  

Characteristics:

- short trajectories  
- sharp curvature  
- high collapse probability  

Low-altitude motion is structurally unstable.
