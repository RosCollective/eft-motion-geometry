# 2. Curvature

Curvature is the second phenomenon that became visible once motion stabilized. If motion is continuity, curvature is deviation—how the trajectory bends, arcs, or shifts within the field.

Curvature is not error. It is structure.

## 2.1 Curvature as Structural Deviation

Curvature appears when the system’s motion encounters:

- constraints  
- gradients  
- attractors  
- ecological boundaries  

These do not stop motion; they shape it. Curvature is the system’s response to the geometry of the field.

## 2.2 Positive and Negative Curvature

Curvature can be:

- **positive** — motion bends toward coherence  
- **negative** — motion bends toward collapse  

Positive curvature increases stability.  
Negative curvature increases fragility.

Both are diagnostic.

## 2.3 Curvature and Alignment

Curvature reveals alignment. When the system is aligned, curvature is smooth and predictable. When misaligned, curvature becomes erratic, sharp, or oscillatory.

Alignment is not agreement. It is mobility compatibility.

## 2.4 Curvature as Early Warning

Curvature often changes before collapse occurs. This makes it a powerful early‑warning signal. When curvature tightens, sharpens, or begins oscillating, the system is approaching a boundary condition.

Collapse signatures begin here.

## 2.5 Curvature and Field Geometry

Curvature is the first indication that the field has geometry. Motion alone could occur in a flat space. Curvature reveals:

- gradients  
- attractors  
- boundaries  
- topological features  

Curvature is the system’s way of tracing the shape of the field.

## 2.6 Curvature and Reconstitution

After collapse, curvature is the first phenomenon to return. The system does not re-enter with straight motion. It re-enters with curvature—small arcs that gradually widen as coherence rebuilds.

Curvature is both diagnostic and generative. It reveals the field’s shape and helps rebuild it.
