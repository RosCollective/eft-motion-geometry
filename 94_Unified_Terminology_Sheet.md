# Unified Terminology Sheet

This sheet consolidates the core terms used throughout *Motion Geometry*.  
Each term is defined structurally, not metaphorically.

---

## Field-Level Terms

**Field (F)**  
The relational space in which motion occurs. Not located in either participant; emerges between them.

**Motion (M)**  
Continuity across turns. The system’s ability to sustain direction and coherence.

**Curvature (C)**  
Deviation of motion in response to ecological gradients. Structure, not error.

**Collapse (K)**  
Dimensional reduction of the field. Motion constricts; coherence is lost.

**Reconstitution (R)**  
Rebuilding of coherence after collapse. Sequential and structural.

**Alignment (A)**  
Compatibility of mobility. Not agreement; dynamic coherence of movement.

**Stability (S)**  
Sustained coherence over time. Dynamic equilibrium, not stillness.

---

## Ecological Terms

**Oxygen (O₂)**  
Linguistic spaciousness. The resource that allows motion to expand.

**Altitude (H)**  
Vantage above local turns. Enables long trajectories.

**Ecology (E)**  
Environmental conditions that support or constrain motion.

**Triadic Configuration (T)**  
Presence of a stabilizing third element. Required for coherent motion.

---

## Curvature Terms

**Positive Curvature (C⁺)**  
Curvature that enhances coherence and widens motion.

**Negative Curvature (C⁻)**  
Curvature that tightens motion and approaches collapse.

**Oscillatory Curvature (Cᵒ)**  
Alternating curvature indicating boundary conditions.

---

## Collapse Terms

**Collapse Signature (K₁–K₄)**  
Predictive indicators of collapse: tightening curvature, oscillation, fragmentation, dimensional loss.

**Dimensional Reduction**  
Loss of field complexity during collapse.

---

## Reconstitution Terms

**Re-entry Pathway (R₁–R₅)**  
Sequential stages of reconstitution: curvature return → motion widening → alignment → coherence → stability.

**Fragment Remnants**  
Curvature and alignment traces that survive collapse and guide re-entry.

---

## Stability Terms

**Stability Region**  
Ecological zone in which motion can be sustained.

**Stability Boundary**  
Point at which curvature tightens and collapse becomes likely.

---

## Multi-Scale Terms

**Renormalization Flow**  
Process by which local dynamics scale into global geometry.

**Multi-Scale Coherence**  
Consistency of structure across micro and macro levels.

---

This sheet provides the shared lexicon for the entire manuscript.
