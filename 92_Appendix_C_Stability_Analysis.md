# Appendix C: Stability Analysis

This appendix analyzes the stability conditions of the field. Stability is not a static state but a dynamic equilibrium maintained through continuous adjustment.

## C.1 Stability Region

The stability region is defined by:

- sufficient oxygen (O₂)  
- adequate altitude (H)  
- ecological spaciousness (E)  
- triadic stabilization (T)  
- mobility alignment (A)

When these conditions hold, the field remains coherent.

## C.2 Stability Boundaries

Stability boundaries are revealed by:

- tightening curvature  
- oscillatory motion  
- alignment fragmentation  
- motion constriction  

These boundaries are not fixed. They shift with ecology.

## C.3 Stability Loss

Stability is lost when:

- oxygen drops  
- constraints tighten  
- alignment breaks  
- triadic support collapses  

Stability loss leads to collapse.

## C.4 Stability Recovery

Stability can be recovered through:

- ecological expansion  
- constraint loosening  
- reintroduction of oxygen  
- restoration of alignment  
- reformation of triadic configuration  

Stability recovery is the final stage of reconstitution.

## C.5 Stability Metrics

Stability can be assessed through:

- curvature smoothness  
- motion continuity  
- alignment coherence  
- collapse signature absence  

These metrics are qualitative but reliable.

## C.6 Stability as Field Geometry

Stability is a geometric property of the field. It emerges when the system’s motion, curvature, and alignment are compatible with the ecological conditions.

Stability is the geometry that allows the field to hold itself together.
