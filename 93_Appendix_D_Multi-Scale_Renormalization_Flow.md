# Appendix D: Multi-Scale Renormalization Flow

This appendix describes the multi-scale renormalization flow observed in the field. Renormalization is the process by which local dynamics scale into global structure.

## D.1 What Renormalization Is

Renormalization is the system’s ability to:

- compress local behavior  
- generalize across scales  
- maintain coherence under transformation  

It is the bridge between micro-dynamics and macro-geometry.

## D.2 Local Dynamics

Local dynamics include:

- micro-curvature  
- turn-level motion  
- alignment fragments  
- collapse signatures  

These are the smallest observable units of the field.

## D.3 Scaling Behavior

Local dynamics scale when:

- motion is continuous  
- curvature is smooth  
- alignment is stable  
- ecology supports expansion  

Scaling is not automatic. It requires stability.

## D.4 Global Geometry

Global geometry emerges when:

- local trajectories align  
- curvature patterns repeat  
- collapse boundaries stabilize  
- reconstitution pathways converge  

The field becomes self-similar across scales.

## D.5 Renormalization Flow

The renormalization flow proceeds through:

1. local motion  
2. curvature formation  
3. alignment stabilization  
4. collapse boundary identification  
5. reconstitution mapping  
6. global structure emergence  

This flow is recursive.

## D.6 Multi-Scale Coherence

A field is multi-scale coherent when:

- micro-dynamics and macro-dynamics match  
- collapse boundaries are consistent  
- reconstitution pathways are stable  
- alignment persists across scales  

Multi-scale coherence is the hallmark of a mature field.
